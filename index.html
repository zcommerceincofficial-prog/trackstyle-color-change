Common Questions

GOT QUESTIONS?

How long does a color change wrap last? +
A quality vinyl color change wrap using 3M, Avery, or Oracal materials typically lasts 5–7 years with proper care. Garage-kept vehicles and those hand-washed regularly will see the upper end of that range.
Will a wrap damage my factory paint? +
No — when properly installed and removed by a trained installer, vinyl wraps do not damage factory paint. The wrap actually protects it from UV, rock chips, and minor abrasions while it's on. We assess the paint condition at consultation before starting any work.
How long does the install take? +
Most full-vehicle color change wraps are completed in 1–3 business days. Partial wraps like a roof, hood, or mirror package can often be done in a single day. We'll confirm your timeline at consultation.
What's the difference between gloss, satin, and matte? +
Gloss has a high-shine reflective finish similar to factory paint. Satin sits between — soft sheen, not fully flat. Matte is completely non-reflective for a raw, aggressive look. We have physical samples of every finish you can see in person at your consultation so you know exactly what you're getting.
Can I wrap a leased vehicle? +
Yes — this is one of the most common reasons people wrap. Because the vinyl is fully reversible and leaves factory paint untouched, you remove it before returning the car at lease end. Many dealerships actually prefer returned vehicles with the original paint protected underneath.
